∑ Mathematical Foundation

Polynomial interpolation finds a polynomial \(P_{n-1}(x)\) of degree at most \(n-1\) that passes exactly through \(n\) given data points \((x_0,y_0),\ldots,(x_{n-1},y_{n-1})\).

Newton's form is built incrementally using divided differences as coefficients, making it efficient for adding new points without full recomputation.

General Newton Polynomial
\[P_{n-1}(x)=f[x_0]+\sum_{k=1}^{n-1}f[x_0,\ldots,x_k]\prod_{j=0}^{k-1}(x-x_j)\]
Divided Difference Recurrence
\[f[x_i]=y_i\] \[f[x_i,x_{i+1},\ldots,x_{i+k}]=\frac{f[x_{i+1},\ldots,x_{i+k}]-f[x_i,\ldots,x_{i+k-1}]}{x_{i+k}-x_i}\]
