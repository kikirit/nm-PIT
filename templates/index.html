Mathematical Concept

Polynomial interpolation is the method of finding a polynomial of degree at most \(n-1\) that passes exactly through a given set of \(n\) data points.

Unlike Lagrange's formulation, Newton's Divided Differences constructs the interpolating polynomial incrementally. This makes it computationally efficient when adding new data points because existing terms do not need to be recomputed.

The Interpolation Formula

The interpolating polynomial of degree \(n-1\) is represented as:

\[P_{n-1}(x) = a_0 + a_1(x-x_0) + a_2(x-x_0)(x-x_1) + \dots + a_{n-1}(x-x_0)\cdots(x-x_{n-2})\]

Where the coefficients \(a_k\) are the **divided differences** defined recursively:

\[a_k = f[x_0, x_1, \dots, x_k]\] \[f[x_i] = y_i\] \[f[x_i, x_{i+1}] = \frac{f[x_{i+1}] - f[x_i]}{x_{i+1} - x_i}\] \[f[x_i, x_{i+1}, \dots, x_{i+k}] = \frac{f[x_{i+1}, \dots, x_{i+k}] - f[x_i, \dots, x_{i+k-1}]}{x_{i+k} - x_i}\]
